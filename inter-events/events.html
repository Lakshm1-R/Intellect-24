Intercollege Events

SHARK TANK


What It Takes: Pitch presentation, supporting documents, and prototyping resources.
Rules: Team size limited to 4 members. Advance submission of startup ideas required. Each team pitches to judges, focusing on clarity and engagement. Evaluation criteria include innovation, feasibility, market potential, and execution ability.

DIGIBYTE


(Evaluate their skills in digital logic and Verilog programming)

Round 1 - Decrypt Hunt: Participants tackle a quiz on basic digital logic, solving problems for numerical values that decode into a word using ASCII characters.
Round 2 - Verisynth: Bring a laptop with stable internet. Teams will log into EDA Playground for circuit simulations and draft code on paper before simulating it.

CIVITAS


Round 1 - Link Up: Participants analyze a set of civil engineering images to determine the corresponding terms. Correct answers earn points. (30-minute time limit)
Round 2 - Civil Discourse: Divided into two teams, participants prepare arguments on a civil topic. The debate includes alternating speeches and rebuttals, with points for clarity and respect. (30-minute time limit)
Round 3 - Model Masters: Participants create a scale model of a civil engineering structure using provided materials, judged on accuracy and creativity. (2-hour time limit)

TECH TREK


Round 1 - APTI-QUEST: Participants solve general aptitude questions covering quantitative reasoning, logic, and verbal skills.
Round 2 - TECH-TRIVIA: Teams face technical questions on programming, algorithms, and data structures.
Round 3 - CODE CLASH: Participants tackle competitive programming problems in real-time to test coding skills.

MR.WORKSHOP


Round 1 - BRAND BRILL: Participants identify car and bike brand logos within a 20-minute time limit.
Round 2 - WORKSHOPGEEK: Shortlisted participants answer workshop-based questions specific to their academic year.
Round 3 - WORKSHOP RIZZ: Finalists present a product based on a given design using workshop or lathe tools.

ELECTROGEN


Round 1 - Connectopia: Teams design a circuit in 15 minutes using a provided block diagram or problem statement.
Round 2 - Error Hunt: Teams identify and correct errors in a circuit and redesign it to produce the correct output within 45 minutes.
